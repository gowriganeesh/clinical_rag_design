Over the past month the patient experienced an asthma exacerbation triggered by a respiratory infection, requiring a short course of oral corticosteroids. Peak flow readings dropped to roughly 70% of personal best during the episode and have since recovered. The patient reports nighttime symptoms approximately twice per week and uses the rescue inhaler about three times per week, which suggests suboptimal control. Spirometry showed mild obstruction with significant bronchodilator reversibility.

Known triggers include pollen, dust, and cold air. The patient is a non-smoker and works in an office environment. There is no history of hospitalization or intubation for asthma. The patient is allergic to penicillin.

Plan: step up controller therapy, review inhaler technique, provide an updated asthma action plan, and arrange allergy follow-up before the next pollen season.
