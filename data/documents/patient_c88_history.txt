Patient is a 45-year-old who underwent laparoscopic cholecystectomy for symptomatic gallstones with recurrent biliary colic. The procedure was uncomplicated, and the patient was discharged the following day. The current presentation is a two-week post-operative review.

The surgical sites are healing well with no signs of infection, erythema, or discharge. The patient reports mild incisional discomfort managed with paracetamol and has discontinued the opioid analgesia prescribed at discharge. Bowel function has returned to normal and the patient is tolerating a regular diet without nausea or fat intolerance. There is no fever, jaundice, or abdominal distension.

Pre-operative liver function tests had been mildly elevated, and a repeat panel is now within normal limits. The patient has resumed light activity and is advised to avoid heavy lifting for another two weeks. There are no known drug allergies.

Plan: continue wound care, support a gradual return to full activity, and arrange routine follow-up only if symptoms recur.
